Total reflection

Let's have another look at Snell's law. This time, we'll write it isolating \(\theta_t\)

$$ \sin{\theta_t} = \dfrac{n_i}{n_t} \sin{\theta_i} $$

We know that the maximum value for the sine of an angle is \(1\). However, if \(n_i > n_t\), there are values of \(\theta_i\) such that leads to \(\sin{\theta_t} > 1\). In that case, experiments show that there are no refracted beam: total reflection occurs. The largest incidence angle for which there is a refracted beam is called critical angle:

$$ \sin{\theta_c} = \dfrac{n_t}{n_i} $$

Note that this phenomenom only occurs when light goes to an environment with a smaller refractive index. This effect is used for example in the transmission of information via optical fiber.
