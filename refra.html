Refraction

Refraction occurs when a wave of any kind reaches an interface that separates two environments with diferent refractive indexes.

Refractive index

The refractive index \(n\) of a material is defined as the ration between the light speed in the vacuum \(c\) and the light speed in that material \(v\). Known that, you can say that the refractive index measures how difficult it is for light to propagate.

$$ n = \dfrac{c}{v} $$

There are two laws of reflection that describe the behavior of the reflected beam.

  1. Both incident and reflected rays are on the same plane.
  2. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

The transmitted beam, on the other hand, makes a different angle with the normal line. The relation between the incidence angle and the transmitted one is described by Snell's law.

Snell's law

When a wave goes from a environment with refractive index \(n_i\) to another one with index \(n_t\), if the angle of incidence is \(\theta_i\) and the angle of transmission is \(\theta_t\), it is true that

$$ n_i \sin{\theta_i} = n_t \sin{\theta_t} $$
